Three Priorities. All Grounded in What the First 60 Days Are Showing.

Priority 1 — Close the Onboarding-to-Vertical-Usage Gap

The biggest opportunity I see is the drop-off between onboarding completion and first meaningful vertical action. The funnel as I estimate it today: Install → Onboarding completion (~61%) → First vertical action → Repeat vertical usage → Cross-vertical usage (~18% of active cohort). Almost 4 in 10 users who install never complete onboarding. Of those who do, many never take a first action. This is not a UA problem — it is a product and onboarding problem. Before scaling spend, I want to understand exactly where users are dropping and why. The cross-vertical signal reinforces this: users who reach more than one vertical are retaining significantly better. Getting users to that moment changes retention fundamentally.

Priority 2 — Nurture the Cross-Vertical Users Already in the Cohort

The 18% of active users already using more than one vertical are a high-value segment even at this early stage. I would focus on understanding what triggered that cross-vertical behaviour — was it in-app prompting, organic discovery, or a specific sequence of actions? Once understood, I would build that trigger into the standard onboarding path. This does not require product development budget. It requires BI analysis and one focused product experiment.

Priority 3 — Build the Phase 2 Go/No-Go Framework Before the Data Lands

By Day 60–90, enough cohort data exists to start shaping the Phase 2 recommendation. I would not wait until all signals are green to start that conversation. I would draft the go/no-go criteria now, align with Product, Finance, and leadership on what "good enough to scale" looks like, and have the framework ready before the data matures. That way the recommendation is a structured decision — not a reactive one made under time pressure.
