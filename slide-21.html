The Phase 2 Decision Is Not a Single Number. It Is a Multi-Signal Read.

By Day 60–90, enough data will exist to make a Phase 2 recommendation. The framework is built around three scenarios — no single metric tells the full story.

Scenario A — Phase 2 Unlock

Condition: D7 ROAS trending toward target AND D7/D30 retention directionally in line with US baseline AND onboarding completion improving AND cross-vertical usage growing.

→ Expand spend to full Phase 2 channel mix. All four signals trending right together is the unlock — not any one alone.

Scenario B — Controlled Phase 2

Condition: Two or three signals are positive but one is lagging — most likely onboarding completion or cross-vertical adoption.

→ Expand cautiously on confirmed channels. Run the onboarding fix in parallel — do not wait for it to resolve before moving, but do not scale past it either.

Scenario C — Pause and Fix

Condition: Retention is significantly below US baseline AND onboarding completion has not improved AND cross-vertical usage is flat.

→ Do not scale spend into a broken funnel. Run a structured product audit and return to the go/no-go framework at Day 90 with a root cause and fix already in flight.

Why This Framework

ROAS, retention, onboarding completion, and cross-vertical usage each measure a different dimension of health. A product can show acceptable ROAS but poor retention. It can show good retention but a broken onboarding funnel that caps growth.

A recommendation built on one metric will miss what the others are telling you.

"No single metric makes this call."
